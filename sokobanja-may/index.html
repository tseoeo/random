Hiking routes

1 · Lepterija → Soko Grad → Golemi Kamen

4.9 km out-and-back-ish (loop possible) · +398 m / −398 m · ~2h 30m · Moderate

Trailhead: Lepterija picnic area, 2 km from Sokobanja — walkable from town or short drive. GPX: Planinarski Savez Srbije (PSS) — pss.rs.

Along the way: Lepterija wooden walkways above the Moravica canyon · the "Virgin Mary in the Rock" (Bogorodica u steni) natural formation · Church of St Mary at Lepterija · Soko Grad fortress — three phases: 6 C Byzantine under Justinian I, 1172 Serbian medieval seat under Stefan Nemanja, destroyed by the Ottomans 1413 · Golemi Kamen + Šiljati Kamen viewpoints over the canyon.

Flora: Paeonia peregrina on basin-edge meadows · Orchis spp. · Iris reichenbachii in limestone niches · Anacamptis pyramidalis late spikes.

Fauna: Grey Wagtail and Dipper on the Moravica · Black Redstart on the fortress walls · Crag Martin on the canyon cliffs · occasional Peregrine Falcon.

2 · Vidikovci viewpoint circuit

7.8 km loop · ~420 m max altitude · ~4 h · Easy-moderate

Trailhead: Sokobanja central square — no car needed. GPX: Sokobanja recreational map — sokobanja.org.rs.

Along the way: Borići, Janior viewpoint, Barudžija spring, Golemi Kamen, Lepterija, Mala Popovica, Banjica — a "best-of" basin-overlook circuit.

Flora: Paeonia peregrina · Salvia pratensis · Leucanthemum vulgare · Lychnis flos-cuculi.

Fauna: Hoopoe · Red-backed Shrike · Cuckoo · Common Swift colonies over town.

3 · Ripaljka waterfall trail

~2–3 km · 1h–1h 30m round trip · Easy

Trailhead: Ozren road, ~5 km from town near Grudno picnic ground — car useful (or a long walk).

Along the way: Ripaljka waterfall — Serbia's first protected natural monument (1949); an 11-tier cascade ~40 m total, highest single tier 11 m, peak flow in late May. Mala Ripaljka (smaller secondary cascade). Ozren forest setting.

Flora: shaded riparian community — ferns, mosses, butterbur (Petasites), Cardamine amara, Aquilegia vulgaris, Geum urbanum.

Fauna: Grey Wagtail · Eurasian Wren · Dipper · woodpeckers in the surrounding forest.

4 · Rtanj Šiljak summit marquee

6.7 km one-way (13.4 km round trip) · +950 m (610 m village → 1,565 m summit) · 5h 45m–6h 30m round trip · Hard

Trailhead: Rtanj village, ~30 min drive from Sokobanja via Rujište or Boljevac. GPX: Komoot Šiljak highlight — komoot.com — and the "take your backpack" trip report.

Profile: forest dirt → rocky "goat trail" → airy ridge walk with low scrambling, open ledges, sudden drop-offs → final summit cone is a short steep scramble to the 1932 St George summit chapel.

Sub-summit variant — no scrambling, no exposure: turn around at the base of the summit cone (~1,400–1,500 m) for the same 360° panorama. 4–5 h round trip, moderate difficulty. Decide at the trailhead.

Along the way: pyramidal Rtanj profile, among Serbia's most iconic mountain forms · limestone meadows · 1932 St George summit chapel · in clear weather, broad views over east Serbia: Stara Planina, Kučaj massif, Ozren, Devica.

Flora: approach forest understorey; mid-slope karst meadows with Iris reichenbachii, orchid groups; summit ridge starting bloom of Gentiana verna (intense blue), Saxifraga on rock, Anemone narcissiflora. Rtanj has 644 documented vascular plant species, 25 endemic. The strict endemic Nepeta rtanjensis sits at 650–850 m on calcareous outcrops but flowers June–July — visible as rosettes only on 22–25 May.

Fauna: Peregrine Falcon · Crag Martin · Alpine Swift · Red-rumped Swallow · Black Redstart on rock faces. Soaring: Short-toed Eagle at peak thermalling activity for reptile hunting · Booted Eagle · Common Buzzard · Long-legged Buzzard · Levant Sparrowhawk. Egyptian Vulture possible (rare). Rock Thrush · Black-eared Wheatear · Northern Wheatear · Tawny Pipit · Woodlark.

Carry 2 L water/person — no springs on the standard route. Warm + wind layer mandatory above 1,200 m.

5 · Ozren-Leskovik 1,174 m

~8 km round trip · +560 m · ~4 h · Moderate

Trailhead: Monastery of St Stephen, Lipovac village — ~25 min drive S. Well-marked every ~20 m, mostly forested. GPX: Planine.net — planine.net.

Along the way: Monastery of St Stephen (Lipovac) at the start; Ozren forest highest point — Ozren's true summit.

Flora: Cephalanthera longifolia · Orchis pallens · Vaccinium myrtillus in flower · late primulas.

Fauna: full woodpecker suite (Black, Great Spotted, Middle Spotted, Green) · Sombre Tit · Red-breasted Flycatcher · Wood Warbler at peak song · Common Redstart · Eurasian Nuthatch · Goldcrest in conifer crowns.

Flora in bloom · late May 2026

Only species in bloom on the trip dates. Nepeta rtanjensis, Satureja kitaibelii, Calluna vulgaris flower June–September and are not listed.

  • Paeonia peregrina (Kosovo peony) — glossy red 10–13 cm flowers, at peak in basin-edge meadows. Headline find. Vidikovci circuit, Lepterija meadows, the recreational circle.
  • Ramonda serbica (Phoenix of the Balkans) — paleoendemic resurrection plant, mauve-purple flowers. 22–25 May catches the very tail of bloom — last chance. On vertical limestone cliffs at Sićevo Gorge (~1 h south via Niš).
  • Iris reichenbachii (Balkan dwarf iris) — purple-blue, dry limestone karst at foothill altitudes. Common on Rtanj approach and Ozren meadows.
  • Dactylorhiza sambucina (elder-flowered orchid) — yellow and purple morphs together; foothill grazed meadows and forest edges.
  • Orchis spp. — multiple terrestrial orchids in basin-edge and foothill meadows.
  • Anacamptis pyramidalis — late spikes possible in limestone grassland.
  • Anacamptis laxiflora — starting its mass-bloom in damp meadows.
  • Syringa vulgaris (wild lilac) — finishing on Rtanj's north slope.
  • Salvia pratensis (meadow clary) — purple-blue, in full bloom in basin meadows.
  • Lotus corniculatus, Trifolium, Knautia arvensis, Linum, Centaurea spp. — limestone-meadow community in active bloom.
  • Aquilegia vulgaris — riverside, Lepterija and Moravica banks.
  • Geum urbanum — riverside.
  • Iris pseudacorus — starting on Bovan reedbeds and wet ditches.
  • Gentiana verna (spring gentian) — intense blue, starting on Rtanj summit ridge (1,400 m +) where snow has just melted.
  • Anemone narcissiflora — starting on Rtanj summit area.
  • Primula veris — late blooms in shaded crevices on Rtanj.

Birds in late May

Best birding sites, in priority order

  1. Bovan Lake — best eBird hotspot in the area, ~72 species reported. Dam and "Velika plaža" for waterfowl; reedbeds at the inflow for warblers; dawn for songbird density.
  2. Rtanj cliffs and ridge — cliff-nesting raptors, summit thermals.
  3. Ozren forest — one of Serbia's largest single-massif forests; full Balkan woodpecker suite and beech-specialty passerines.
  4. Moravica + Lepterija canyon — riparian and small-cliff birds.
  5. Sićevo Gorge (~1 h south, day-trip) — vulture and raptor cliffs.

Headline species in late May

  • Cliff raptors: Peregrine Falcon · Short-toed Eagle (peak thermalling for snake hunting) · Booted Eagle · Levant Sparrowhawk (back from African wintering) · Long-legged Buzzard. Egyptian Vulture possible. Griffon Vulture as wide-ranging forager from Stara Planina.
  • Forest woodpeckers (Ozren): Black · Great Spotted · Middle Spotted (beech/oak) · Green · Grey-headed · Lesser Spotted.
  • Forest songbirds (Ozren beech-pine): Sombre Tit (Balkan specialty) · Red-breasted Flycatcher (Balkan beech specialty) · Collared Flycatcher · Wood Warbler at peak song · Common Redstart · Garden Warbler · Hawfinch possible.
  • Wetland (Bovan reedbeds): Great Reed, Reed, Sedge Warbler all in song · Marsh Harrier quartering · Squacco Heron, Night Heron in margins · Hobby hawking dragonflies at dusk.
  • Bovan open water: Great Crested Grebe · Black-necked Grebe · Little Grebe · Common Tern · Mallard · Gadwall · Coot · Yellow-legged Gull.
  • Cliffs and rocks (Rtanj, Lepterija): Crag Martin · Alpine Swift · Red-rumped Swallow · Black Redstart · Rock Thrush · Black-eared Wheatear.
  • Basin and farmland: Hoopoe (vocal in early morning) · European Bee-eater colonies excavating bank cuttings on the Moravica banks — late May arrival peak · European Roller (less common) · Red-backed Shrike · Lesser Grey Shrike · Corn Bunting · Quail at dawn/dusk · Turtle Dove · Cuckoo at peak calling.
  • Riparian (Moravica, Ripaljka): White-throated Dipper · Kingfisher · Grey + White Wagtail · Common Sandpiper.
  • Dusk owls: Eurasian Eagle Owl on the Soko Grad canyon · Tawny Owl in Ozren forest · Scops Owl in town gardens.

History & culture

Sokobanja town centre

  • Amam Turski (Ottoman hamam) — bathing site in continuous use since the Roman period, remodelled by the Ottomans, documented by Evliya Çelebi in 1663, state-protected.
  • Church of the Transfiguration of the Lord — 19 C central parish church.
  • Hajduk Veljko Memorial Fountain at Vrelo-Borići.

Within 5 km

  • Soko Grad fortress (2 km E, above the Moravica canyon) — three building phases: 6 C Byzantine under Justinian I, 1172 Serbian medieval seat under Stefan Nemanja, destroyed by the Ottomans in 1413. Declared a Monument of Culture of Great Importance in 1982.
  • Lepterija picnic area — wooden walkways above the canyon, the "Virgin Mary in the Rock" natural formation, small Church of St Mary, all trail-linked to Soko Grad.
  • Ripaljka waterfall — Serbia's first protected natural monument (1949), 11-tier cascade.

Within 15 km

  • Jermenčić Monastery on Ozren — founded 1392 by Armenian deserters from the Ottoman army after the Battle of Kosovo (1389); dedicated to Archangels Gabriel and Michael; primary feast 26 July (outside trip).
  • Gradašnica watermills — 13 early-19 C mills on the Gradašnica stream; one restored for tourism.
  • Lake Bovan — 1977 artificial reservoir.

Within 30–60 min

  • Vrmdža (Vrmdžanska Kula) fortress — Roman-era under Justinian I, destroyed 1413, 12 km N.
  • Jošanica Church — founded 11 C, 15 km NW; "the oldest existing church in eastern Serbia."
  • Sesalac Cave — 19 km E toward Knjaževac. 200 m lit visitor trail with stalactites/stalagmites/travertine tubs, small underground river. ~4 km gravel approach.

Day trips

Felix Romuliana / Gamzigrad

UNESCO World Heritage Site, ~1h 13m drive N. Late-Roman fortified palace built by Emperor Galerius (late 3 C – early 4 C). Often called the most preserved imperial palace in Europe. Modern visitor centre on-site. Worth a half-day.

Niš

~55 min drive W. Niš Fortress (Ottoman atop Roman/Byzantine/medieval layers), Skull Tower (Ćele Kula, 1809), and Mediana — Constantine's 3 C imperial residential palace complex; reopened to visitors in 2023 after years closed; cash entry only — bring RSD.

Sićevo Gorge Nature Park

~1 h drive S via Niš. Pair with the Ramonda serbica bloom and karst-cliff raptor watching. Bogorodica Sićevačka monastery (1644) on the gorge rim. Sićevo Art Colony — founded 1905 by Nadežda Petrović, oldest art colony in the Balkans.
