Cycling routes

Four routes. The Giro del Rtanj is the headline; Bovan is the rest-the-legs day; Ozren circular keeps you in deep forest; the basin circle is a 15-km bailout.

A · Giro del Rtanj bike

33.5 km loop +600–800 m ~60 % gravel · ~40 % asphalt traffic: very low

Direction: Sokobanja → Vrmdža → Jošanica → Pakleš → Vasiljevac → Glavica → Sokobanja.

Along the way:

  • Vrmdžanska Kula fortress — Roman-era walls under Justinian I, destroyed 1413.
  • Jošanica Church (~11C) — described as "the oldest existing church in eastern Serbia."
  • 19C operating watermill at Jošanica.
  • Rtanj pyramid as constant backdrop — the 1,565 m near-perfect natural pyramid.

Flora:

Paeonia peregrina (peak) Orchis spp. Anacamptis laxiflora Salvia pratensis limestone meadow community

Fauna:

Hoopoe European Bee-eater (colonies on Moravica banks) Red-backed Shrike Cuckoo (peak) Common Buzzard

GPX source: Bike Adventure Belgrade · BK Gorski. Local file is a corridor approximation.

42.3 km loop +300–400 m ~80 % paved · ~20 % macadam traffic: light

Direction: Sokobanja → SW down Moravica → Trubarevac → Bovan dam crossing → N shore via Aleksinac-side connectors → back.

Along the way:

  • Bovan reservoir — 1977 artificial lake, 8 km long, max depth ~50 m, motorboats prohibited.
  • Hacijenda Gane at Trubarevac — lakeside lunch stop.
  • Dam structure — concrete arch dam, infrastructure-tourism viewpoint.

Flora:

Aquilegia vulgaris (riverside) Iris pseudacorus (reedbeds starting) Salvia pratensis Centaurea spp.

Fauna:

Hiking routes

Five hikes spanning easy town-walks to Rtanj's hard summit cone. The sub-summit Rtanj variant gives the same 360° panorama without the scramble.

1 · Lepterija → Soko Grad → Golemi Kamen hike moderate

4.9 km out-and-back-ish +398 m ~2h 30m trailhead: Lepterija (walkable from town)

Along the way:

  • Lepterija — wooden walkways above the Moravica canyon.
  • "Virgin Mary in the Rock" (Bogorodica u steni) — natural rock formation on the right-bank cliff.
  • Church of St Mary at Lepterija.
  • Soko Grad fortress — three phases: 6C Byzantine (Justinian I), 1172 Serbian medieval (Stefan Nemanja), destroyed 1413.
  • Golemi Kamen + Šiljati Kamen viewpoints — limestone outcrops over the canyon.

Flora:

Paeonia peregrina Orchis spp. Iris reichenbachii Anacamptis pyramidalis (late spikes)

Fauna:

Grey Wagtail Dipper Black Redstart (fortress walls) Crag Martin Peregrine (occasional)

GPX source: Planinarski Savez Srbije.

2 · Vidikovci viewpoint circuit hike easy-moderate

7.8 km loop ~420 m max alt ~4 h trailhead: Sokobanja central square (no car)

Along the way: Borići, Janior viewpoint, Barudžija spring, Golemi Kamen, Lepterija, Mala Popovica, Banjica — a "best-of" basin overlook circuit.

Flora:

Paeonia peregrina Salvia pratensis Leucanthemum vulgare Lychnis flos-cuculi

Fauna:

Hoopoe Red-backed Shrike Cuckoo Common Swift (town colonies)

GPX source: Sokobanja recreational map.

3 · Ripaljka waterfall trail hike easy

~2–3 km 1–1.5 h round trip trailhead: Ozren road near Grudno (car or bike)

Along the way:

  • Ripaljka waterfall — Serbia's first protected natural monument (1949). 11-tier cascade, ~40 m total, highest tier 11 m. Peak flow in late May.
  • Mala Ripaljka — smaller secondary cascade.
  • Ozren forest setting.

Flora:

Petasites (butterbur) Cardamine amara Aquilegia vulgaris Geum urbanum ferns + mosses

Fauna:

Grey Wagtail Eurasian Wren Dipper Woodpeckers in surrounding forest

4 · Rtanj Šiljak summit hike hard

13.4 km round trip +950 m (610 m → 1,565 m) 5h 45m – 6h 30m trailhead: Rtanj village (~30 min drive)

Forest dirt → rocky "goat trail" → airy ridge walk with low scrambling, open ledges, sudden drop-offs → final summit cone is a short steep scramble to the 1932 St George chapel.

Sub-summit variant — no scrambling, no exposure

Turn around at the base of the summit cone (~1,400–1,500 m) for the same 360° panorama. Becomes ~4–5 h round-trip, moderate difficulty. Pick at the trailhead based on the day's weather and how the ridge feels.

Along the way:

  • Pyramidal Rtanj profile — among Serbia's most iconic mountain forms.
  • Limestone meadows.
  • 1932 St George summit chapel at the top (Šiljak).
  • Clear-weather views: Stara Planina, Kučaj massif, Ozren, Devica.

Flora:

Iris reichenbachii (karst meadows) orchid groups (mid-slope) Gentiana verna (ridge starting) Saxifraga on rock Anemone narcissiflora

Rtanj has 644 documented vascular plant species, 25 endemic. The Rtanj-strict endemic Nepeta rtanjensis sits at 650–850 m on calcareous outcrops but flowers June–July only — visible as rosettes, not in flower 22–25 May.

Fauna:

Peregrine Falcon Crag Martin Alpine Swift Red-rumped Swallow Black Redstart Short-toed Eagle (peak thermalling) Booted Eagle Long-legged Buzzard Levant Sparrowhawk Egyptian Vulture (rare) Rock Thrush Black-eared + Northern Wheatear Tawny Pipit Woodlark

Carry 2 L water per person — no springs on the standard route. Warm + wind layer mandatory above 1,200 m.

GPX source: Komoot Šiljak · Take Your Backpack trip report.

~8 km round trip +560 m ~4 h trailhead: Monastery of St Stephen, Lipovac (~25 min drive S)

Well-marked every ~20 m, mostly forested all the way to Ozren's true summit.

Along the way: Monastery of St Stephen (Lipovac) at the start; Ozren forest highest point.

Flora in bloom in late May 2026

Only species actually flowering 22–25 May. Headline endemics that flower June–September (Nepeta rtanjensis, Satureja kitaibelii, Calluna vulgaris) are deliberately skipped.

The trophies

  • Paeonia peregrina peak — Kosovo peony, glossy red 10–13 cm flowers, at peak bloom in basin-edge meadows. Best on the Vidikovci circuit, Lepterija meadows, and the Sokobanja recreational circle.
  • Ramonda serbica paleoendemic · tail of bloom — Phoenix of the Balkans, resurrection plant, mauve-purple flowers. 22–25 May catches the very tail of its window — last chance. Found on vertical limestone cliffs at Sićevo Gorge (~1 h south via Niš) — worth the day-trip for botanical interest.

Foothill & karst meadows

  • Iris reichenbachii — small purple-blue Balkan dwarf iris on dry limestone karst; common on Rtanj approach and Ozren karst meadows.
  • Dactylorhiza sambucina — elder-flowered orchid, yellow + purple morphs together in foothill grazed meadows.
  • Orchis spp. — multiple terrestrial orchids in basin-edge and foothill meadows.
  • Anacamptis pyramidalis — late spikes possible.
  • Anacamptis laxiflora — starting its mass-bloom period in damp meadows.
  • Syringa vulgaris — wild lilac finishing on Rtanj's north slope.

Basin-meadow community

  • Salvia pratensis — purple-blue, full bloom.
  • Lotus corniculatus, Trifolium, Knautia arvensis, Linum, Centaurea spp. — limestone-meadow community in active bloom.

Riparian

  • Aquilegia vulgaris — Lepterija and Moravica banks.
  • Geum urbanum — riverside.
  • Iris pseudacorus — starting on Bovan reedbeds and wet ditches.

Rtanj summit ridge (1,400 m+)

  • Gentiana verna — spring gentian, intense blue, starting where snow has just melted.
  • Anemone narcissiflora — starting on the summit area.
  • Primula veris — late blooms in shaded crevices.

Birds in late May

Past spring migration peak, deep into breeding season — dawn (05:00–08:00) and pre-sunset (18:30–20:30) drive most ID work.

Best birding sites (in priority order)

  1. Bovan Lake — best eBird hotspot in the area with ~72 species reported. Dam and "Velika plaža" for waterfowl, reedbeds at the inflow for warblers, dawn for songbird density.
  2. Rtanj cliffs and ridge — cliff-nesting raptors, summit thermals.
  3. Ozren forest — one of Serbia's largest single-massif forests; full Balkan woodpecker suite and beech-specialty passerines.
  4. Moravica + Lepterija canyon — riparian and small-cliff birds.
  5. Sićevo Gorge (1 h south, day-trip) — vulture and raptor cliffs.

Headline species in late May

  • Cliff raptors: Peregrine Falcon, Short-toed Eagle (peak thermalling for snake hunting), Booted Eagle, Levant Sparrowhawk (back from African wintering), Long-legged Buzzard. Egyptian Vulture possible. Griffon Vulture as wide-ranging forager from Stara Planina.
  • Forest woodpeckers (Ozren): Black, Great Spotted, Middle Spotted (beech/oak), Green, Grey-headed, Lesser Spotted.
  • Forest songbirds (Ozren beech-pine): Sombre Tit (Balkan specialty), Red-breasted Flycatcher (Balkan beech specialty), Collared Flycatcher, Wood Warbler at peak song, Common Redstart, Garden Warbler, Hawfinch possible.
  • Wetland (Bovan reedbeds): Great Reed Warbler, Reed Warbler, Sedge Warbler all in song. Marsh Harrier quartering. Squacco Heron, Night Heron in margins. Hobby hawking dragonflies at dusk.
  • Bovan open water: Great Crested + Black-necked + Little Grebe, Common Tern, Mallard, Gadwall, Coot, Yellow-legged Gull.
  • Cliffs and rocks (Rtanj, Lepterija): Crag Martin, Alpine Swift, Red-rumped Swallow, Black Redstart, Rock Thrush, Black-eared Wheatear.
  • Basin and farmland: Hoopoe (vocal in early morning), European Bee-eater colonies excavating bank cuttings on the Moravica banks — late-May arrival peak. European Roller (less common), Red-backed + Lesser Grey Shrike, Corn Bunting, Quail (dawn/dusk), Turtle Dove, Cuckoo at peak calling.
  • Riparian (Moravica, Ripaljka): White-throated Dipper, Kingfisher, Grey + White Wagtail, Common Sandpiper.
  • Dusk owls: Eurasian Eagle Owl on the Soko Grad canyon, Tawny Owl in Ozren forest, Scops Owl in town gardens.

History & culture

Sokobanja town centre

  • Amam Turski — Ottoman hamam, in continuous use since the Roman period, remodelled by the Ottomans, documented by Evliya Çelebi in 1663, state-protected.
  • Church of the Transfiguration of the Lord — 19C central parish church.
  • Hajduk Veljko Memorial Fountain at Vrelo-Borici.

Within 5 km

  • Soko Grad fortress (2 km E, above the Moravica canyon) — three building phases: 6C Byzantine (Justinian I), 1172 Serbian medieval (Stefan Nemanja), destroyed by the Ottomans 1413. Monument of Culture of Great Importance (1982).
  • Lepterija picnic area — wooden walkways, "Virgin Mary in the Rock," small Church of St Mary, trail-linked to Soko Grad.
  • Ripaljka waterfall — Serbia's first protected natural monument (1949).

Within 15 km

  • Jermenčić Monastery on Ozren — founded 1392 by Armenian deserters from the Ottoman army after the Battle of Kosovo (1389); dedicated to Archangels Gabriel and Michael; primary feast 26 July (not during the trip).
  • Gradašnica watermills — 13 early-19C mills on the Gradašnica stream; one restored for tourism.
  • Lake Bovan — 1977 artificial reservoir.

Within 30–60 min

  • Vrmdža (Vrmdžanska Kula) fortress — Roman-era under Justinian I, destroyed 1413, 12 km N.
  • Jošanica Church — founded 11C, 15 km NW; "the oldest existing church in eastern Serbia."
  • Sesalac Cave — 19 km E toward Knjaževac; 200 m lit visitor trail with stalactites/stalagmites/travertine tubs, small underground river. ~4 km gravel approach.

Day trips

Felix Romuliana / Gamzigrad

~1h 13m drive N · UNESCO World Heritage

Late-Roman fortified palace built by Emperor Galerius (late 3C – early 4C). Often called the most preserved imperial palace in Europe. Modern visitor centre on-site. Worth a half-day.

Niš

~55 min drive W

Niš Fortress (Ottoman atop Roman/Byzantine/medieval layers), Skull Tower (Ćele Kula, 1809), and Mediana — Constantine's 3C imperial residential palace complex, reopened to visitors in 2023 after years closed.

Mediana entry is cash-only. Bring RSD or convert at the border / in Niš.

Sićevo Gorge Nature Park

~1 h drive S via Niš · botanical + birding

Combine with the Ramonda serbica bloom (tail-end this week — last chance) and karst-cliff raptor watching. Bogorodica Sićevačka monastery (1644) on the gorge rim. Sićevo Art Colony (founded 1905 by Nadežda Petrović) — oldest art colony in the Balkans.

Logistics

Drive from Sofia

225 km / 2h 45m via E80 motorway through Kalotina border, A4 to Niš (1h 48m motorway), then R-247 east through Aleksinac and the Moravica valley (~55 min).

Border timing — Cyril & Methodius long weekend (Sun 24 + Mon 25 May are BG holidays)

Friday 22 May outbound: Kalotina spikes in the afternoon. Leave Sofia before 11:00 or after 21:00.

Monday 25 May return: Leave Sokobanja before 13:00 or after 19:00. The peak return wave hits Kalotina mid-late afternoon.

Mountaineering event — Saturday 23 May

Sokobanja's official calendar lists "Mountaineering Action Small Rtanj's Peaks" on May 23, organised by MA "Oštra Čuka". Rtanj-area trailheads (Rtanj village, Vrmdža) will be busier than usual that morning; parking tighter; you may meet groups of 30–100 mountaineers on standard routes.

Two options if you want to do Rtanj Šiljak:

  • Start before 07:00 to clear the climb ahead of the group.
  • Move Rtanj to Sunday or Monday; spend Saturday on non-Rtanj activities (Lepterija + Soko Grad + Bovan, or the Felix Romuliana day-trip).

Cash, water, coverage

  • Cash: Mediana entry in Niš is cash-only (RSD). Carry some euros and convert at the border or in Niš.
  • Water: No springs on the Rtanj summit route — carry 2 L per person.
  • Mobile coverage: good in town, intermittent on Rtanj ridge, none in Sesalac cave.
